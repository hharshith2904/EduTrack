I. General Principles:
**Respect and Courtesy:**
- All users (students, faculty, administrators) must treat each other with respect and courtesy, regardless of their role or position. 
 Language used in feedback should be constructive and avoid personal attacks or insults. 
**Academic Integrity:**
- Feedback should be honest and accurate, reflecting genuine experiences and perspectives. 
- Plagiarism or fabrication of feedback is strictly prohibited. 
**Confidentiality and Privacy:**
- Student feedback will be treated as confidential and used only for the purpose of improving teaching and learning. 
- Students have the right to privacy, and their identities will be protected unless they choose to disclose them. 
"Purpose of Feedback:"
- The feedback system is intended to be a tool for improvement and should be used constructively. 
- Feedback should focus on teaching methods, course content, and the overall learning environment, not on personal attacks or inappropriate comments. 
II. Student Conduct:
**Honesty and Accuracy:**
- Students are expected to provide honest and accurate feedback. 
- Falsifying or fabricating feedback is a serious violation of the code of conduct. 
**Respectful Language:**
- Students must use respectful and appropriate language in their feedback. 
- Avoid using abusive, offensive, or discriminatory language. 
**Privacy and Confidentiality:**
- Students should be aware that their feedback may be shared with faculty or administrators, but their identities will be protected unless they choose to disclose them. 
- Students should not share or disclose the feedback of others without their permission. 
**Appropriate Use of the System:**
- Students should use the feedback system only for its intended purpose: to provide feedback on teaching and learning. 
- Students should not use the system to harass, intimidate, or defame others. 
III. Consequences of Misconduct:
**Minor Violations:**
- Students who engage in minor violations of the code of conduct may receive a warning or other form of counseling.
**Serious Violations:**
- Students who engage in serious violations of the code of conduct may face disciplinary action, including suspension or expulsion.
**Specific Examples:**
- Plagiarism or fabrication of feedback
- Use of abusive, offensive, or discriminatory language
- Harassment or intimidation of others
- Violation of privacy or confidentiality
- Misuse of the feedback system for personal gain or malicious purposes. 
IV. Implementation and Enforcement:
**Clear Communication:**
- Code of conduct should be clearly communicated to all users of the feedback system. 
- The code of conduct should be easily accessible and available in multiple formats (e.g., online, printed). 
**Training and Education:**
- Faculty and administrators should be trained on how to use the feedback system effectively and how to respond to concerns or complaints. 
- Students should be educated on the purpose and proper use of the feedback system. 
**Enforcement:**
- A clear process for reporting and investigating violations of the code of conduct should be established. 
- Disciplinary actions should be consistent and fair.
